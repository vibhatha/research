# Understanding SLBFE Meeting Protocols

## **Board of Directors: Composition and Appointments**
The Bureau is managed by a **Board of Directors** (the "Board") consisting of **eleven members** appointed by the Minister. The composition of the Board is as follows:
*   **One member** appointed in consultation with the Minister in charge of **Finance**.
*   **One member** appointed in consultation with the Minister in charge of **Foreign Affairs**.
*   **One female member** appointed in consultation with the Minister in charge of **Womenâ€™s Affairs**.
*   **Eight other members**, of whom **four must be representatives of licensed foreign employment agencies**.

**Terms and Disqualifications:**
*   **Disqualification:** Any person who is a **Member of Parliament** is disqualified from being appointed or continuing as a Director.
*   **Tenure:** Every Director holds office for **two years** unless they resign, die, or are removed earlier.
*   **Re-appointment:** Directors vacating office by resignation or expiration of their term are **eligible for re-appointment**.
*   **Removal:** The Minister may remove any Director from office **without assigning a reason**. A Director removed from office is ineligible for re-appointment or to serve the Bureau in any other capacity.
*   **Remuneration:** Members are remunerated at rates determined by the Minister with the concurrence of the Minister in charge of Finance.

## **Board Meetings: Participation and Procedures**
The sources establish specific protocols for the conduct of Board meetings:
*   **Quorum:** The quorum for any meeting of the Board is **five members**.
*   **Chairman's Role:** The Minister appoints the Chairman from among the Directors. The Chairman **presides at all meetings**.
*   **Absence of Chairman:** If the Chairman is absent, the Directors present must **choose one of their members to preside** at that meeting.
*   **Voting:** The Chairman (or the presiding member) has a **casting vote** in addition to his original vote.
*   **Procedural Authority:** Subject to the provisions of the Act, the Board is empowered to **regulate its own procedure** regarding meetings and the transaction of business.
*   **Validity of Acts:** No act or proceeding of the Board is deemed invalid solely due to a **vacancy** among the Directors or a **defect in the appointment** of any Director.

## **Meeting Content and Statutory Obligations**
During Board meetings, certain specific duties and disclosures must be performed:
*   **Disclosure of Interest:** If a Director is directly or indirectly interested in any contract made or proposed by the Bureau, they must **disclose the nature of that interest** at a meeting of the Board. 
*   **Recording of Minutes:** Such disclosures **must be recorded in the minutes** of the Board.
*   **Recusal:** The Director who has disclosed an interest **must not take part in any deliberation or decision** of the Board regarding that contract.
*   **Workers' Welfare Fund Reports:** At **each meeting of the Board**, the Chairman is required to submit a **statement of receipts and disbursements** on account of the Workers' Welfare Fund.
*   **Delegation of Powers:** The Board may use its meetings to **delegate powers or duties** to the Chairman, a Director, or an employee for the purpose of efficient administration.
*   **Administrative Management:** The Board meets to exercise and perform the powers and duties of the Bureau, including the administration of its affairs.
